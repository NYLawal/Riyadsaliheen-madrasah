What We Offer

Standards-based Arabic and Islamic education where excellence meets affordability

Adult Madrasah

A weekend madrash for male adults. We have classes for primary (ibtidahi) and idaadi (junior secondary) learners.

Our weekend classes are suitable for both working-class adults and business owners. We believe that Islamic education has several benefits. Age should never be a barrier.

  • Class Days: Saturdays and Sundays
  • Time: 4:30pm - 6:45pm

Female Madarasah

A weekend madrasah for mothers and young women. Designed to suit women's divine duties and obligations.

We recognise women as home and nation builders who play a very important role in forming a healthy society. Women need to acquire Islamic education in order to build such society.

  • Class Days: Fridays and Sundays
  • Time: 2:40pm - 4:00pm (Fridays)
  •            2:00pm - 4:00pm (Sundays)

Full-time Program

A weekday full-time madrasah for teenagers and young adults. Classes are available for both primary and secondary levels.

Also suitable for school leavers and potential undergraduates who are preparing to face the challenges of the outside world.

  • Class Days: Mondays - Fridays

Our Courses

We teach subjects that cut across aspects of religious upliftment, moral upbringing and Arabic language comprehension

Hifzul Qur'an

Students learn to engage deeply with the verses of the Qur'an through memorisation. It sharpens the brain with the skills necessary for learning and memory-based tasks.

Tawheed

Students acquire the knowledge of Allah in terms of His Lordship, worshipping Him, and His names and attributes. Tawheed is the foundation upon which Islam has been built.

Fiqh

Students acquire knowledge about Islamic legal rulings from their sources, and clear guidance on how to lead a good and meaningful life in accordance with Islamic teachings.

Hadith

Students develop a deeper understanding of the Prophet's character and his teachings, which in turn helps them develop their own character and become better Muslims.

Lugha

It provides students with a strong, firm foundation in the understanding and usage of the Arabic language, with proper Arabic conversations.

Tajweed

Students learn the proper pronunciation and recitation of the Quran, enhancing recitation clarity and beauty, and deepening spiritual connection with the Quran.

Nahw

Students learn the relationship between words in a sentence and the rules that govern such arrangement to convey meaning. They learn the structure of Arabic sentences and how they're formed.

Sarf

Students learn the formation and transformation of words, creating familiarity with Arabic verbs, an awareness of their conjugation, and the ability to recognise their patterns.

Tadribaatu Lugha

Students learn another approach to combining the building blocks of the Arabic language, through a practical-oriented format and real-life demonstration of established conversations.

Ta'abeer

Students learn about Arabic conversations, how to construct expressions in Arabic and speak it fluently; as well as grasping the understanding of conversational texts.

Tafseer

Students learn the proper understanding of the verses of the Qur'an through their meaning and explanation. It helps them reflect on the wisdom, ethics, and morals conveyed in the text.

Imlah

Students rehearse their spelling, writing and sentence formation skills by learning to recognise, spell and write Arabic letters in the different ways and instances in which they can appear.

Qiraaha

Students learn and practice Arabic language reading. Reading helps to develop proficiency in Arabic, and strengthens all the other skills (listening, speaking, and writing)

Taajiyah

It helps students just learning Arabiyyah to develop multiple linguistic skills at once; including handwriting and the ability to match sounds-to-letters to spell words.

Khatu

Students learn the writing of the single, initial, median and end part of Arabic letters. They also articulate the sound the letter(s) make, to ease pronunciation.

Adhkaar

Students learn a series of invocations uttered for the remembrance of Allah, as prescribed by our beloved Prophet Muhammad.
