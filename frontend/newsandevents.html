  • Arabic Presentation

A student giving his presentation in Arabic

During one of our termly activities, one of the students who presented, Abulllah AbdulHakeem, spoke about the etiquettes of a student of knowledge. His speech focused on three aspects, which include the etiquettes of the students of knowledge to themselves, to their teachers and to the knowledge they are seeking.

Among his points were the necessity of a student to be patient with his studies, to dress neatly always, avoid distractions and refrain from haram. He also mentioned that a student should respect his teachers, show humility towards them and correct them with respect and good manners whenever they make a mistake. He went further to state that it is important for students to revise their lessons frequently, seek explanation or help when needed, and not focus too much on matters where scholars have difference of opinion.

He concluded by backing up his points with verses of the Qur'an, authentic hadith and popular sayings from some scholars of Islam, such as Imam Shaf'i. We ask Allah to grant him and all the other students blessings in their knowledge.

Judges seated during the termly co-curricular activity

Mid-Term Test

Test and assessments are part of learning. They are never a threat to students or a kind of punishment.

Our mid-term test is a form of assessment, and assessments generally are meant for measuring students' understanding of the lessons which they have taken over time. In our madrasah, assessments are a usual thing, as students are tested almost weekly to keep track of their progress and where they are lagging behind.

The mid-term test however is a formal way of giving the students themselves and their parents or guardians a more tangible view of their progress. They get to see for themselves where they are doing well and where they need to put in more effort.

Please check the term calendar for when this will happen for this term, as we encourage parents and guardians to ensure their wards are always brushing up on what is learnt and always ready for assessments, even if a particular date isn't assigned for such.

Your Children Are Your Business...

Alhamdulillah, the madrasah system and legacy that we enjoy with freedom in our country is a blessing that cannot be enumerated at all. In many parts of the world, learners are deprived of furthering their Islamic Education or at the very least, they are limited to an hour a week of Islamic Education.

When our children make the transition to the last level of high school, at times we tend to choose the so-called easier option of withdrawing them from the madrasah. This is due to the notion that our children will be unable to cope with high school and madrasah, or we have given preference to extra-curricular activities over Islamic education. In reality, this thought process is very detrimental to the child, as many Islamic aspects, injunctions and life lessons are taught in the madrasah.

The need for our children to continue their Islamic education cannot be over-emphasised. The onslaught of western ideologies and other agendas that are foreign to Islam, are being taught in some schools and the society, with no Islamic perspective whatsoever, and this has a major negative impact on our children. Added to that, the influence and challenges of the modern-day world through social media and other means, puts our learners at a disadvantage.

Hence, my humble appeal to our respected parents is that we should not rush in removing our children from the madrasah once they're about to finish high school. Instead, let them continue furthering their Islamic knowledge, that which the benefits will be acquired, both in life and once our worldly existence comes to an end.

What Exactly Do We Mean By Peace?

For Muslims, peace is not merely the absence of war or organized violence. It is also the presence of justice and the creation of conditions in which humans can realize their full potentials.

Critics of Islam often brand it as a barbaric and brutal religion. Their argument is generally based on a select few ayaat (Quaranic verses) and hadith (sayings of the Prophet Muhammadﷺ), which seem to promote violence.

There are indeed a number of ayaat and hadith which encourage Muslims to turn to combat in certain situations. However, it is important to remember, that much of the Quran was revealed during a time of war. The vast majority of seemingly pro-violence ayaat and hadith were revealed to Prophet Muhammadﷺ while his followers were being persecuted by those who did not believe his message.

The word Islam indicates the very essence of the religious system known by that name. Its primary significance is the “making of peace,” and the idea of peace is the dominant idea in Islam. Islam stands for peace between man and his Creator, between man and his fellow beings and between different religions and communities. Peace is the greeting of one Muslim to another and “Peace” shall also be the greeting of those in Paradise.
